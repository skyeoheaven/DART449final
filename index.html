Artist Statement

This is a project for Concordia’s DART449 course. Much of this course focuses on the different ways that JavaScript can be used for website design, and seeing so many interesting functions being used in scroll-based interactions struck a chord with me.

I have been a lifelong comic book fan, and I have a decent physical collection, but much of my comic reading has been done on a screen. I saw an opportunity with my final project for this class to attempt to elevate the traditional storytelling of comic books using scroll-based elements. I had always liked the idea of somehow combining comics with music for an elevated experience, and practicing event listeners was the perfect way to try this. Although I wish I had more time to refine the final product, I managed to fit in many of the creative ideas I was interested in trying, and I am proud of the outcome.

I plan on revisiting and expanding this project further. I believe that with more practice and refinement, I can produce a scroll-based storytelling experience that pushes the boundaries of traditional comic storytelling. The combination of dynamic visuals, smooth scrolling, and synchronized audio offers a unique way to tell a story that is both engaging and immersive.
